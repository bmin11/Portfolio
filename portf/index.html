Purpose Statement

The current version of the portfolio is written to accommodate the criteria listed by the MGMT 1001 - Managing Organizational Issues course at Dalhousie University. The main purpose of this portfolio to organize all computer science related works into a single site, which can be used as both a record of my learning progression and a resource to judge my capability as a software developer. I hope to use this portfolio as a reference whenever I feel in doubt of this path and to re-ignite my interest in software development field. Additionally, the portfolio will include my works on MGMT assignments as an evidence of the course completion. These assignments will be used as a self-reflection on my past actions.

Philosophy Statement

True knowledge exists in knowing that you know nothing. -Socrates

I may sound full of myself for quoting Socrates, but I truly believe I should remind myself of this quote as often as possible. There are few things I can say with confidence and one of them is my ignorance on any given subjects. I may have some knowledge to participate in a conversation or draw a reasonable assumption on a cause and a solution to a problem. However, I should never consider my opinion as a definite information and have an open ear to other’s word. Being able to accept that my opinion is wrong and correct it is, I believe, the most crucial to becoming a better person. It is also important to not be afraid of sharing opinions, despite knowing it is likely to be wrong. The best way to find out what is wrong with the opinion is to openly share and ask others opinion about it.

“Knowing is not enough, we must apply. Willing is not enough, we must do.” -Bruce Lee

This is what I need to work on. There is little value if I do not act as I thought and yet, I still do not act as what I believe is the best, and just surrender myself to what is most convenient.

Introduction

The purpose of this portfolio is to organize my completed projects during my computer science studies and become a hub for reflections on my progress. Firstly, I will introduce myself and describe what I have done up to this point. Secondly, the main portion of the portfolio will consist of my projects with a screenshot of the program and either a small description or a full documentation. Each projects will have a link to the GitHub for code review. Finally, there will be a section with my MGMT assignments done during the 16/17 year. It will mainly serve as a self-reflection.

To any potential reader other than myself, I hope this portfolio could serve as a reference to both my progress as a student and my ability as a software developer. It may not cover my personal life, as I much prefer to not share my life story, but I sincerely hope this portfolio can represent me as a student pursuing the computing industry.
